How to Trade

How To Trade

We recommend that you start trading using our risk-free Quick Demo account. With $10,000 in virtual funds, you can practise trading while you learn how to use our platform and familiarise yourself with all our features and the binary option assets and timeframes available on HIGHLOW.

When you're ready to place a trade, simply select an option and decide in which direction you think the Binary Option will expire: higher or lower than the purchase price. Choose how much you want to trade and click the ‘Invest’ button. Congratulations! You have now traded a binary option.

A walkthrough of the trading process with HighLow:

  • Step 1. Select an Option.

    ① The option carousel displays all available options with current price, payout and expiry time. Select the option you wish to trade either by using the carousel arrows, the asset group tabs, or the dropdown menu.

    The option will now be displayed on the chart.

  • Step 2. Purchase an Option

    Firstly, select a direction HIGH or LOW. This can be done either by ② clicking on the HIGH or LOW arrow on the chart, or ③ clicking the HIGH or LOW arrow on the trade panel below the chart.

    ④ Enter your purchase amount. This can be done by manually entering an amount, or by choosing one of the pre-defined amounts. ⑤ Click on the ‘Invest’ button to submit your trade.

  • Step 3. View your option

    ⑥ The details of your position will be displayed in the ‘Trade Actions’ window below the trade panel. This window can be expanded to show more than 5 trades by clicking on the icon.

  • Step 4. Closing your Option

    The trader has the ability to close a position early if so desired. There are two possible ways of closing a position. ⑦ Click on the ‘Sell’ tab (of the position you wish to close) in 'My Trade Actions', and then ⑧ click ‘Sell’ again to submit the order to close your position. You can also click the trade marker (of the position you wish to close) on the chart, and then click on 'Sell' to submit the order to close your position.

    If you choose to close a position prior to its expiry time, the payout will be reduced by the amount of the 'break cost'. The break cost cannot be determined in advance by HighLow but, when it determines the break cost, HighLow will, in good faith, have regard to the time left to the expiry of the option, the volatility of the market price of the binary option’s underlying security and general market conditions.
